A little about me...

Creative | Practical | Intuitive

Communication is the key to success in any line of work. Being able to understand your team, you clients, and objectives while being able to adequately communicate your ideas, your work and even your obstacles is important. For me, design is another form of communication. It’s visual, it’s impactful and it should be meaningful.

With a very diverse background that ranges from military service to health care and customer service to design, I feel that I have a unique perspective on communication, which can bring an eclectic set of skills to any team and/or project. My interests and career focus are in:

  • Web Design
  • UI/UX Design
  • Inforgraphic Design
  • Social Media Marketing
  • Graphic Design

With a degree in Graphic Design and working toward certifications in UX Design, Web Design and Computer Science, my hope is to obtain an inhouse design position, where I can help contribute to the companies’ success with dedication and creativity as a valued member of a team.

Syndeo was a specially designed access portal for PAML clients and physicians to have direct access to their patient information. Built internally, and maintained by PAML IT, this system offered up to date test results, code changes and exclusive product and testing updates.

AION | UI/UX Redesign

AION, another PAML service, worked specifically with physicians practicing age management, by offering them services and custom testing panels to assist them in providing the best care for their clients.

With an increase in demand and advancements in both testing and reporting technologies, the AION Physicians Portal had become outdated. PAML wanted to offer the AION physicians more options, online, and update the portal to support the need growing functionality.

While maintaining the modern esthetic of the AION Laboratories site, the physicians portal needed to be intuitive to the physicians, so that they would feel comfortable with such drastic changes, and require minimal re-training on the services they had become accustomed to.
